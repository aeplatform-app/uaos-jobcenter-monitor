# Agent C Demo Checklist Cleanup

Status: LOCAL ONLY

Purpose: provide a clean checklist for Jobcenter/supporter screenshots and local/static demos without changing app source, demo code, hosting configuration, or deploy files.

## Required Safety Labels

Use these labels on demo notes, screenshot captions, and handoff text:

- LOCAL ONLY.
- NOT PUBLIC RELEASE.
- Static/local demo reference.
- Private presentation aid.
- No public service is active.
- No payment or live customer use is active.
- No real keyboard writer/export is active.
- No external automation is active.

## Jobcenter Screenshot / Demo Checklist

Before sending or showing a Jobcenter-facing pack, verify:

- The screenshot or demo note clearly says LOCAL ONLY.
- The screenshot or demo note clearly says NOT PUBLIC RELEASE.
- The owner can explain that the demo shows project direction and current capability planning.
- The material frames hardware/workstation/laptop needs as project enablement needs.
- The material keeps funding discussion tied to milestones.
- Any demo gateway link is described as local/static/private only.
- Any product wording uses prototype, planning, or presentation language.
- No screenshot claims public launch.
- No screenshot claims production readiness.
- No screenshot claims payment readiness.
- No screenshot claims live customer use.
- No screenshot claims real keyboard writer/export readiness.
- No screenshot claims ownership of third-party samples, Kontakt, Native Instruments, or proprietary libraries.

## Supporter / Friend Screenshot / Demo Checklist

Before sending or showing a supporter/friend pack, verify:

- The material is described as private and local.
- The request is for feedback, practical support, or project understanding.
- The owner can explain what is working now and what remains planned.
- The material does not invite public sharing.
- The material does not present a public URL as a live product.
- The material does not ask anyone to treat the demo as customer-ready.
- Any support request is framed around equipment, documentation, presentation polish, or careful next steps.
- Public release, payment, real export, and external automation remain blocked.

## What Each Demo Proves

Local/static demo materials may prove:

- The project has organized planning and documentation.
- The owner has a coherent AE Platform / UAOS product direction.
- Local demo pages and screenshots can explain the idea.
- Safety gates are documented and active.
- Presentation assets are being prepared carefully.
- Future work can be divided into small bounded tasks.

Local/static demo materials do not prove:

- Public release.
- Production readiness.
- Live hosting readiness.
- Payment readiness.
- Live customer readiness.
- Real keyboard writer/export readiness.
- Proprietary library ownership.
- External automation readiness.

## What Must Not Be Claimed

Do not claim:

- The product is launched.
- A production service is running.
- A public deployment exists.
- Payment or subscriptions are active.
- Real arranger keyboard files are generated.
- Kontakt, Native Instruments, or proprietary samples are included or owned.
- The GitHub transfer is completed.
- External automation is approved.
- Background, open-ended, or 24-hour agents are running.

## Final Send-Readiness Notes

Safe to send only when:

- The material is clearly local/private.
- The owner can explain current limitations in plain language.
- Screenshots do not imply a live public service.
- All risky claims are removed or replaced with blocked/not-active wording.
- The current remote remains unchanged.
- The owner is comfortable that the recipient will not treat the demo as a public release.

Stop and revise when:

- A recipient might think the demo is live.
- The page text suggests public release, production readiness, payment, or real export.
- Any proprietary library ownership claim appears.
- Any source-code or hosting change would be needed.
